Project 04 / Operations software

Primo Roster

A constraint-solving staff rostering system built around the real availability, award rules and manager feedback of a working restaurant.

ContextFamily restaurant
EngineOR-Tools CP-SAT
Timeline2026 - present
StatusPilot / live roster
The brief

Turn a recurring manual scheduling problem into a solvable constraint system.

Restaurant rostering has competing requirements: staff availability, business coverage and award compliance. Primo Roster translates those real rules into a weekly schedule and lets manager feedback become new constraints rather than disappearing into ad-hoc edits.

Architecture

Optimisation at the core, human feedback around it.

Business inputsAvailability, coverage needs and award rules.
Constraint solverGoogle OR-Tools CP-SAT generates a feasible roster.
Manager feedbackAn LLM loop interprets notes as new constraints.
Roster UIReact frontend served by a FastAPI backend.
Why it matters

A production-minded project, not a demo dataset.

  • Use a formal solver. CP-SAT is suited to hard and competing scheduling constraints.
  • Keep managers in the loop. Natural-language feedback becomes structured system input.
  • Separate responsibilities. FastAPI handles the backend while React provides the operating interface.
  • Test with real work. The system runs against an actual weekly restaurant roster.
Current status

The system is in pilot use. The next useful evidence for this page is quantified time saved, roster feasibility rate and examples of constraints resolved without manual rework.
