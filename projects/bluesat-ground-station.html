Project 01 / Space systems

BlueSat Ground Station

Mechanical design of an antenna-pointing system for communication with a student-built CubeSat in low Earth orbit.

RoleMechanical Lead
TeamBlueSat UNSW
Timeline2025 - present
StatusIn development
The brief

Turn orbital communication requirements into buildable hardware.

The ground station needs mechanical hardware that supports antenna pointing while integrating with electrical control and software. My responsibility is the mounts and bracketry around that pointing assembly, together with cross-team mechanical coordination.

Primary toolSolidWorks
Engineering contextMultidisciplinary space system
System view

Mechanical design sits inside a larger control loop.

Satellite passOrbital target creates pointing demand.
ControlSoftware and electrical systems command motion.
Pointing hardwareMounts and brackets carry and locate the antenna.
CommunicationThe antenna links with the student CubeSat.
My contribution

Design, iterate and integrate.

  • Own the mechanical package. Lead mount and bracket design for the antenna pointing assembly.
  • Iterate in CAD. Develop and refine geometry in SolidWorks as system requirements evolve.
  • Coordinate interfaces. Work across electrical, software and mechanical subteams toward shared launch milestones.
  • Support the team beyond CAD. Help run outreach events promoting space engineering at UNSW.
Current status

The ground-station system remains in development. The current focus is mechanical iteration and multidisciplinary integration; performance results will be added after testing.
